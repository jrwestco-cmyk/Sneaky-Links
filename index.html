Blog of the Week: Hyperion Max 3

The Hyperion Max 3 from Brooks Running has shocked the running community
around the world. The brand has always been reserved with creating daily trainers
for their suburban joggers and people who are on their feet all day. So when
Brooks Running launched their Hyperion Series a few years ago we had an idea that
there might be something different in the brands future. This year they unveiled it
with this shoe. Here is what you need to know; weight: 9.9 oz. (280 g) for a
US M9, 8.8 oz. (250 g) for a US W8, stack height and drop: 46 mm in heel,
40 mm in forefoot (6 mm drop), and price: $200.

When Brooks Running sent me this shoe and I pulled it out of the box I was
surprised how tall it was. With a stack height of 46 mm this shoes does
not disappoint with lack of cushioning. When I tried them on for the first
time and took a lap around the house I felt unstable because I have never worn
a running shoe with that much height before. I immediately took them off and
placed them back in the box until further notice. I was afraid I was going to
roll an ankle if I took a turn too fast or worse fall on my face due to how soft
the shoe felt under my foot. As a runner who has returned from multiple running
injuries too many thoughts filled my head that could go wrong with this shoe.

After about a week I saw the box again with the shoes in it and thought I should
try them again and take them for a run. Just a short run in the neighborhood,
nothing crazy. Well, that run turned into a few miles. I felt like I was flying
and in reality I was. I was running my mile times 2-3 minutes faster than I normally
do. I assumed it was all in my head and a shoe couldn't have that kind of power.

The reality of it is, Brooks Running designed this shoe for speed. With a responsive
speed fault plate underfoot paired with race day foam… Some are calling it “Runners Gold”.
After giving this shoe a second chance I can see what all the hype is about. This shoe
will cost you around $200 which is the same value as other running shoes that are ranked
in the same performance category. So if you are in the market for a speed shoe go try it
on at your local running store, I would love to hear your thoughts on it!
